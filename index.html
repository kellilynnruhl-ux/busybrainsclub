A cozy place to start, focus, and finish.

Body doubling is simple: we gather, name what we are working on, and spend focused time doing our own tasks in supportive company. Research on accountability, co-regulation, and social facilitation suggests many brains focus more easily when another person is present. No pressure to perform. No shame spiral. Just enough structure to help your brain begin.

How it works

Each group begins with a quick hello and a simple goal shared in the chat. Cameras stay on to support presence and accountability, while microphones remain muted during the full hour of focused work time. At the end, everyone shares progress in the chat and we send you off with a warm goodbye. The goal is to make getting started feel easier and more doable. Sometimes a little shared presence is exactly what the nervous system needs to settle in and begin.

Questions you might be wondering.

Do I have to keep my camera on?

Yes. Cameras stay on during sessions to support connection, accountability, and the body doubling experience. Audio stays muted during focus time unless otherwise invited.

What kinds of tasks can I bring?

Anything that helps your life move forward: paperwork, dishes, studying, emails, creative projects, cleaning, planning, or resting intentionally.

Is this therapy?

No. These groups are for structure, encouragement, and shared focus. They are not a substitute for therapy or mental health treatment.
